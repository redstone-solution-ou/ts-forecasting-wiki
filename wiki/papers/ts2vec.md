# TS2Vec: Towards Universal Representation of Time Series

> **Short name:** `ts2vec` · **arXiv:** [2106.10466](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.10466) · **PDF:** [local](../../papers/ts2vec_2106.10466.pdf) · **Date:** 2021-06 (v4: 2022-02) · **Venue:** AAAI 2022

**Authors:** Zhihan Yue, Yujing Wang, Juanyong Duan et al. (Peking University & Microsoft)

## Abstract
TS2Vec is a self-supervised framework that learns timestamp-level representations of time series via hierarchical contrastive learning over augmented context views. A 10-block dilated CNN encoder is trained so that representations at the same timestamp under two augmentations (timestamp masking + random cropping) are pulled together, while other timestamps in the same series (temporal contrast) and the same timestamp in other batch series (instance contrast) are pushed apart. The loss is applied recursively after max-pooling along time, yielding a single encoder usable at any granularity. The authors evaluate on UCR-128 / UEA-30 classification, four ETT/Electricity forecasting splits, and Yahoo/KPI anomaly detection, reporting substantial improvements over prior unsupervised TS representations on all three tasks.

## Key contributions
- **Hierarchical contrasting** along both the instance axis (other series in the batch at the same `t`) and the temporal axis (other timestamps in the same series), with the loss summed over `O(log T)` scales generated by recursive `maxpool1d(kernel=2)` along time (Algorithm 1, Equations 1–3).
- **Contextual consistency** as a positive-pair definition: two augmented views from Bernoulli(0.5) timestamp masking plus a random pair of overlapping crops `[a1,b1], [a2,b2]`, with the loss applied only on the overlap `[a2,b1]`. Argued (§2.3, Figure 3) to be more robust than subseries consistency under level shifts and temporal consistency under anomalies.
- **A single encoder for many tasks**: max-pool over time for classification, last-timestamp representation + linear regression for forecasting, masked-vs-unmasked dissimilarity as an anomaly score (Equation 4).
- **+2.4% accuracy on UCR-125 and +3.0% on UEA-29** over the best prior unsupervised TS representation, at training time `0.9 h` / `0.6 h` on a single RTX 3090 vs. T-Loss `38.0 h` / `15.1 h` (paper Table 1).

## Architecture at a glance
A fully-connected input projection lifts each scalar `x_{i,t}` to a latent `z_{i,t}`; Bernoulli(0.5) timestamp masking is then applied on the latents, followed by a 10-block residual dilated CNN with dilation `2^l` at the `l`-th block. Random cropping samples two overlapping windows per step. At each level, temporal contrast (Eq. 1) and instance-wise contrast (Eq. 2) are summed; the representation is `maxpool1d(kernel=2)` down-sampled along time and the loss recurses until length 1 (Algorithm 1). Representation dimension `K = 320`.

Two design rationales make TS2Vec the canonical pre-FM TS encoder. (a) **Contextual consistency** as the positive-pair definition (timestamp masking + random cropping with overlap, paper §2.3 Figure 3) is more robust than the alternatives the prior literature used: *subseries consistency* (T-Loss) breaks under level shifts because the two subseries can have arbitrarily different means, and *temporal consistency* (TNC) breaks when an anomaly spikes inside one of the two windows. Two views of the *same timestamp* under different surrounding contexts naturally absorb non-stationarity without assuming any global invariance. (b) **Hierarchical contrasting** (Algorithm 1, §2.4) applies the loss recursively after `maxpool1d(kernel=2)` downsampling, so a single encoder is forced to maintain consistent representations at multiple time-scales — fine-grained patterns at the bottom levels, coarse instance-wise semantics at the top — which is what enables the same frozen encoder to win on UCR/UEA classification, ETT/Electricity forecasting, and Yahoo/KPI anomaly detection without per-task fine-tuning.

## Why it matters
TS2Vec is the standard reference for "TS-specific [contrastive representation learning](../concepts/contrastive-representation-learning.md)." It crystallizes a four-way split of the positive-pair design space — subseries consistency (T-Loss), temporal consistency (TNC), transformation consistency (TS-TCC), and TS2Vec's own contextual consistency — that later self-supervised TS papers cite verbatim. It is also the canonical TS encoder against which non-reconstructive successors ([TS-JEPA](./ts-jepa.md), [LaT-PFN](./lat-pfn.md), [MTS-JEPA](./mts-jepa.md)) report matched-capacity ablations on UCR/UEA classification.

## Strengths
- Beats prior unsupervised baselines on UCR-125 (TS2Vec `0.830`, T-Loss `0.806`, TS-TCC `0.757`, TNC `0.761`, TST `0.641`) and on UEA-29 (`0.712` vs. TS-TCC `0.682`, TNC `0.677`, T-Loss `0.675`); the Nemenyi 95% Critical Difference diagram (Figure 4) puts TS2Vec significantly ahead in average rank (paper Table 1).
- ~40× faster training than T-Loss on UCR (`0.9 h` vs. `38.0 h`) and 25× faster on UEA (`0.6 h` vs. `15.1 h`), because the multi-scale loss is amortized inside one batch rather than requiring a separate discriminator pass (Table 1 + §3.1).
- The frozen encoder + L2-penalized linear regression on the last-timestamp representation beats Informer / LogTrans / N-BEATS / TCN / LSTnet on univariate ETT/Electricity at horizons 24–720 — average MSE `0.209` vs. Informer `0.310` (Table 2), with lower training and inference time than Informer (Table 3).
- Robust to up to 50% missing timestamps with negligible UCR accuracy loss on the four largest datasets (Figure 6); the ablation isolates this gain to hierarchical contrast + timestamp masking.
- Clean ablation (Table 5): swapping dilated CNN for LSTM costs `−5.0%`, for Transformer `−18.2%`; replacing contextual consistency with subseries / temporal consistency costs `−4.9%` / `−2.2%`. Adding jitter / scaling / permutation augmentations *hurts*, supporting the paper's argument against transformation-invariance for TS.

## Limitations and open critiques
- The 10-block dilated CNN has a bounded receptive field and a fixed parameter budget; no scaling study and no claim of foundation-model-scale generalization. TS2Vec is a per-dataset encoder, not a pretrained TS-FM.
- Per-instance contrastive negatives treat each multivariate series as one entity; the loss does not exploit cross-variate structure (electricity meters, retail SKUs), one motivation for the channel-mixing designs in [Chronos-2](./chronos-2.md) and [Moirai](./moirai.md).
- The benchmark suite (UCR/UEA, ETT, Yahoo/KPI) skews short-context, single-frequency, and largely univariate. Long-horizon multivariate forecasting and probabilistic evaluation (CRPS / WQL) are out of scope.
- Forecasting and anomaly heads sit downstream of frozen representations; the paper does not finetune the encoder per task, so the headroom from end-to-end finetuning is unmeasured.
- Random cropping and Bernoulli(0.5) masking are tuned implicitly via UCR validation; no sensitivity analysis is reported on the mask probability or crop-overlap distribution.

## Follow-up work and dialogue
TS2Vec is the standing reference for non-reconstructive self-supervised TS encoders. [TS-JEPA](./ts-jepa.md) runs a matched-capacity head-to-head on FordA classification (`91.5%` vs. TS2Vec `86.4%`) and other UCR tasks, framing JEPA's EMA-target prediction as a substitute for InfoNCE-style negatives. [LaT-PFN](./lat-pfn.md) reports zero-shot UCR-128 numbers that beat TS2Vec despite TS2Vec being trained on those datasets — evidence that pretrained-on-prior > trained-on-task at sufficient corpus scale. [MTS-JEPA](./mts-jepa.md) keeps TS2Vec as a baseline in the multivariate anomaly-prediction setting. Upstream, TS2Vec inherits the InfoNCE recipe from [CPC](./cpc.md), replacing CPC's GRU aggregator + future-step prediction with a hierarchical CNN + same-timestamp contrast; the bridge is laid out in [contrastive representation learning](../concepts/contrastive-representation-learning.md).

## Reproducibility
- **Open artifacts:** code at `https://github.com/yuezhihan/ts2vec`.
- **Code:** public.
- **Data:** UCR-128, UEA-30, ETTh1 / ETTh2 / ETTm1 / Electricity (forecasting), Yahoo and KPI (anomaly) — all public; splits follow Franceschi et al. (2019) and Zhou et al. (2021).
- **Compute to retrain:** single NVIDIA RTX 3090; total training time `0.9 h` on UCR-125 and `0.6 h` on UEA-29 (Table 1). No multi-GPU run reported.
- **Deployment footprint:** dilated CNN, representation dimension `K = 320`; parameter count is sub-100M and the paper does not give an exact number. Channel-independent; sub-series representations are obtained by max-pool over the corresponding timestamps.

## When to cite this paper
Cite TS2Vec as the canonical reference for hierarchical contrastive learning on time series, for the contextual-consistency framing of positive pairs (timestamp masking + random cropping), and as the strongest TS-specific contrastive baseline in the pre-FM era — particularly on UCR/UEA classification and the narrow ETT/Electricity forecasting suite. Prefer modern TS-FMs ([Chronos](./chronos.md), [Moirai](./moirai.md), [TimesFM](./timesfm.md)) for cross-domain zero-shot forecasting claims; prefer [TS-JEPA](./ts-jepa.md) for the negative-free EMA-target alternative.
